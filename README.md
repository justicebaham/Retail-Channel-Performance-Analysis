**Project 3: Retail Channel Performance Analysis**

**Project Overview:** A comprehensive audit and analysis of omnichannel revenue distribution across 12,000+ transactions. Leveraged SQL window functions to calculate the market share between the online and in store channels and utilized cloud-based visualization tools to present the insights.



**Problem:**

The business needed to determine the revenue contribution of online vs in-store channels across 12,000+ rows of raw transaction data. Before analysis could begin, the data required an integrity audit to ensure all revenue-critical columns were complete to avoid misrepresentation of the insights.



**Action:**

* **Data Quality Audit:** Executed a SQL audit using COUNTIF functions to identify 604 null records in the 'total spent' column and excluded those records to ensure the analysis was based only on verified transaction data.
* **Market Share Calculation:** Executed an SQL query utilizing window functions to calculate the exact percentage of total revenue generated by each channel.
* **Precision Formatting:** Applied the ROUND function to the revenue percentages to provide clean figures for reporting.
* **Visualization:** Migrated the final dataset to Google Sheets to create a pivot table and a pie chart for a simple and effective presentation.



**Result:**

* Confirmed that the 604 null values were not included in the final analysis.
* Discovered a nearly equal revenue split between the channels, with online sales (51%) slightly outperforming in-store sales (49%).
* The balanced split suggests a strong omnichannel presence, though the slight lead from online sales indicates a successful digital growth trend.
* Created a dashboard to effectively visualize the revenue split.






**Tools Used:**

* **SQL (BigQuery):** Data extraction and aggregation.
* **Google Sheets (Connected Sheets):** Data transformation.
* **Data Visualization:** Pie Chart and Pivot Table analysis.
